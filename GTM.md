# 11-0 — Go-To-Market Strategy (India)

**Product:** "11-0 — The All-Time Cricket XI Challenge." A free, no-login, mobile-web game: 11 spins lock a nation + decade, you draft one player per spin into an all-time XI, and a simulation engine plays a full World Cup. Win all 11 matches to go 11-0.

**The play in one line:** Do to Indian cricket WhatsApp what 82-0 did to American basketball Twitter — but engineered for India's distribution channels, not borrowed from the US playbook.

---

## 1. Positioning: sell the wound, not the game

India went **10-1** at the 2023 World Cup. Every Indian fan carries that scar. The entire brand voice leans into it:

- Tagline: **"Kya aapki XI 11-0 jaa sakti hai?"** (Can your XI go 11-0?)
- Loss-the-final result screen literally says *"We know exactly how this feels."* That screenshot IS the marketing.
- Launch line for press/creators: *"India never got its 11-0. Build the XI that finally does."*

This makes the game emotionally specific to India rather than a generic clone, and gives every share an in-joke that 500M people instantly understand.

## 2. Product-led virality (already baked in)

The growth engine is the share artifact, not ads:

1. **WhatsApp-first share button** (`wa.me` deep link) — in India, WhatsApp > Twitter for casual virality. The emoji scorecard (🟢🟢🔴⬜…) is Wordle-grammar: instantly readable, instantly competitive, spoiler-free.
2. **Daily Challenge** — everyone in the country gets the *same 11 spins* each day (date-seeded). This converts the game from a toy into a daily ritual and makes office/family/college WhatsApp groups the leaderboard. "Beat my 8-3 from today's spins" is the core loop.
3. **CricIQ mode** (stats hidden) — the flex mode. Creators and know-it-all uncles play this on camera; failing in CricIQ is funnier content than succeeding in Classic.
4. **Downloadable team poster** (canvas PNG) — a designed, brandable artifact for Instagram Stories/Status. Every poster is an ad with the URL on it. (Later: this becomes the monetization hook — see §8.)

**Target K-factor ≥ 1.2:** every result screen pushes share before replay; "Draft Again" is deliberately *below* the share buttons.

## 3. Launch timing & sequencing

Ride a cricket moment — never launch into a vacuum:

- **T-0 window options (pick nearest):** IPL playoffs week, an India bilateral series, Asia Cup, or T20 World Cup warm-ups. Peak option: the week India loses/wins anything big (engagement spikes 10x).
- **Day 1 (seeding):** r/Cricket post ("I built 82-0 but for cricket — be brutal"), cricket Twitter/X threads, 10 mid-size meme pages with pre-made content. No press yet.
- **Day 2–4 (creator wave):** 30–50 short-form creators go live with the same challenge format (see §4). Daily Challenge starts counting publicly ("Day 3: only 0.4% of India went 11-0 today").
- **Day 5+ (earned media):** pitch the *numbers*, not the game: "X lakh XIs drafted in 96 hours; only Y went 11-0" to Cricbuzz, ESPNcricinfo, News18, Indian Express sports, The Bridge, OTT cricket shows.

## 4. Creator strategy: hand them a format, not a brief

Don't buy "promotions." Give creators a repeatable *format* they'd make anyway:

- **"Beat My XI"** — creator posts their record, dares comments to beat it with the same Daily spins. Infinite duet/stitch fuel.
- **"CricIQ Roast"** — two friends, stats hidden, loser does a forfeit. Perfect for YouTube Shorts/Reels.
- **"Uncle vs Gen-Z"** — dad picks from the 70s/80s spins from memory, kid picks the 2020s. Built-in family content; this is the segment that makes things *nationally* viral in India, not just Twitter-viral.
- **Commentary creators** (the Hindi commentary parody niche) voice-over their own simulation feed.
- **Tiering:** 5 anchor creators (1M+, paid), 50 mid-tier (50K–1M, seeded + bounty for best video), unlimited long tail via a public "creator kit" page (logo, screen recordings, suggested hooks in Hindi/Tamil/Telugu/Bengali).

**The 82-0 endgame, India edition:** the original game won when NBA teams and players played it on main. Our equivalent targets: IPL franchise admins (RCB/CSK/KKR meme-savvy social teams), state association handles, and retired players who are extremely online (Sehwag, Ashwin's YouTube channel, Yuvraj). One Ashwin video = product-market fit announcement.

## 5. Distribution channels most campaigns ignore

- **WhatsApp/Telegram fantasy groups** — millions of Dream11 users sit in prediction groups; the game is a perfect off-day activity. Seed admins directly.
- **ShareChat & Moj** — vernacular-first platforms where English-first campaigns never show up. Translate share text + landing into 6 languages; ship `?lang=` variants.
- **College fests & hostels** — "11-0 Nights": projector, one phone, the whole hostel votes on picks. Provide a free "host a draft night" kit. Campus ambassadors get poster credits.
- **Gully cricket QR drops** — stickers/posters with QR codes at maidans, turf grounds, and box-cricket arenas in Mumbai, Delhi, Bangalore, Chennai: *"Your gully XI vs history. Scan."*
- **Office Slack/Teams** — the Daily Challenge is a perfect 10:30am office ritual; pitch HR/community managers of large IT companies a "company league" spreadsheet template.

## 6. Innovative stunts (cheap, loud, on-brand)

- **"Sack the Selectors" days:** the day after any real India defeat, push a special banner — "Selectors flopped. Your turn." Counter-programming grief with agency.
- **City vs City week:** pin-code captured at share time → "Mumbai's average record: 7-2. Delhi: 6-3. Settle it." Regional rivalry is free fuel.
- **Live "auction" streams:** creators draft live with chat voting on each pick, IPL-auction style, complete with the gavel sound.
- **The 11-0 Wall:** a public page listing every verified perfect XI ever drafted (name + city + the XI). Scarcity = status; people will grind for a spot.
- **Jersey-number easter eggs:** on Kohli's birthday, every spin guarantees one India combo; on July 2 (1996-spirit days), 90s combos rain. Announce nothing; let users discover and post.
- **Bollywood/cricket crossover bait:** "Which actor's XI is this?" quote-tweet games from the brand handle.

## 7. Tech & SEO foundation (built for Bharat)

- **Zero-install PWA, < 300KB total, no frameworks** — loads on a ₹6,000 Android on 3G. This is a feature; say it out loud in marketing.
- Works fully offline after first load (add a service worker before scale pushes).
- **SEO:** own "all time cricket XI game", "cricket 82-0", "11-0 game" queries with the landing copy; FAQ schema; vernacular landing pages for Hindi/Tamil/Telugu keywords.
- Result pages share with OG images so WhatsApp link previews look premium.
- Analytics: track spins started → XIs completed → simulations run → shares tapped (the only funnel that matters).

## 8. Monetization runway (later — do NOT gate anything at launch)

Keep the game 100% free and clean during the growth phase. Then layer in, in order of fan-friendliness:

1. **Affiliate links (Indian programs):** **Amazon Associates India** (amazon.in) and **Flipkart Affiliate** — contextual placements only: drafted Kohli? Result screen offers his bat brand / India jersey. Cricket gear, jerseys, books (e.g., autobiographies of drafted legends). Native, optional, never interstitial.
2. **Victory poster print-on-demand:** users who go 11-0 (or anyone, at a price) can order a **physical framed poster of their XI** — fulfilled via Indian POD partners (Printrove/Qikink), ₹499–999, with the date and "1 of N perfect XIs" serial number. Status object + revenue + offline billboard in someone's room.
3. **Sponsored Daily Challenges:** "Today's spins powered by ___" for fantasy apps, sports brands, or streaming platforms during tournaments.
4. **Never:** paywalls on drafting, ads between picks, or anything that slows the loop below 90 seconds per game.

## 9. Retention & metrics

- **Ritual:** Daily Challenge streaks (🔥 counter), weekly themed drafts ("90s only", "IPL-era only", "Left-handers week").
- **North-star metric:** Daily XIs simulated. Supporting: share rate per result (target 25%+), D1 ≥ 30%, D7 ≥ 12%, WhatsApp CTR on shared links.
- **Week-6 decision gate:** if share rate < 10%, the artifact is wrong — iterate on the result screen before spending another rupee on distribution.

## 10. Risks & mitigations

- **Clone speed:** the 82-0 wave spawned copycats in days. Moats: the Daily Challenge social graph, the 11-0 Wall, vernacular depth, and brand voice. Ship fast, own the name.
- **Name/likeness rights:** we use only player names + factual career references (no photos, no team logos, no endorsement implied) — the established fantasy-sports posture in India. Add a visible disclaimer (done) and get a legal read before monetizing posters that feature player names.
- **Gambling-adjacent perception:** no money in, no money out, no prediction on real matches. State this in the FAQ to stay clearly outside real-money-gaming regulation.
- **Bot/cheat pressure on the 11-0 Wall:** verify perfect runs server-side before the Wall goes live (the current client-only build is fine pre-Wall).

---

**Summary:** the product *is* the funnel — WhatsApp scorecards, a national Daily Challenge, and a poster people actually want. Spend on creators with formats (not ads), launch into a cricket moment, weaponize the 10-1 wound respectfully, and keep monetization invisible until the ritual is formed.
